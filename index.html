Nethra Natarajan

Data Science | Public Policy | LSE

Hi, I'm Nethra Natarajan, a Data Science for Public Policy student at the London School of Economics with an undergraduate degree in Economics and Finance. Born in the US, raised in India, and now studying in the UK, I'm passionate about using rigorous data analysis to inform evidence-based policy, particularly in education, health systems, and social protection.

I work across Python, R, and Stata, with experience in econometrics, spatial analysis, machine learning, quantitative text analysis, and data visualisation. My projects range from analyzing health disparities in India using machine learning to studying gentrification patterns from Airbnb activity in LA through GIS mapping to modeling strategic behavior in digital platforms through game theory. Beyond research, I lead the Womxn's Network at LSE, where we combine evidence-based analysis with advocacy for gender equality.

When I'm not analysing datasets or organising events at LSE, you'll find me singing Indian classical and light music, exploring new cities, or strolling around London finding new parks and museums.
